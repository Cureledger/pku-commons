## Where phe numbers come from

Apps read phenylalanine from USDA FoodData Central. It has measured phe for single ingredients like an egg or an apple. It has almost no phe for packaged, composed foods like a candy bar or a frozen meal. For those, the record is just the Nutrition Facts label: protein rounded to the whole gram, no amino acids. So the app guesses, and different apps guess differently. That is the disagreement you see.

USDA cannot fix this by measuring 350,000 products. The scalable fix is to calculate phe from the recipe, using measured ingredient values, and to say so on every number.

PKU Commons does that. Every phe value carries how it was found, measured, calculated, or estimated, and how far it might be off. Apps get scored on the quality of their data first.
