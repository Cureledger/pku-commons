How a phe estimate is produced

The Skill encodes the method a trained PKU parent uses — made explicit and reviewable.

  1. Countable? Screen the label to the diet-relevant range (≤ 2 g protein per serving), where precise phe counting matters most.
  2. Find phe-bearing ingredients. Parse the ingredient list for items that contribute phe.
  3. Classify by protein source. Assign a phe-per-gram-protein value per ingredient by type.
  4. Apply a recipe factor. Estimate each phe-bearing ingredient's relative weight in the recipe from basic food science.
  5. Scale to portion & log. Multiply by the weighed portion; record for tracking and clinic sharing.
We deliberately constrain the AI's non-deterministic reasoning and add explicit guidance for foreseeable conditions (e.g. weak grocery Wi-Fi, partial labels) — reducing variance so estimates are consistent and defensible.

How accuracy is validated

This is the part built for you: a reproducible standard, not a vendor claim.

Reproducible ground truth. Each benchmark case pairs a food label with a phe value computed from documented public sources — USDA FoodData Central and Open Food Facts — with the source id recorded. You (or anyone) can re-derive the "true" value independently.

Transparent metrics. Every estimator is scored the same way: mean absolute error, per-case error, and the share of estimates falling within a clinically-relevant tolerance band.

Versioned & audited. Methodology rules and food-list rows carry a citation to authority, a version, and a reviewer of record. Your challenges become tracked issues with documented resolutions — an audit trail, not a black box.

Your sign-off has standing. Clinician / RD review is a first-class authority layer: a named professional endorsement is a citable source in the record.
